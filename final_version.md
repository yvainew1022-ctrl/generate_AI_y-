# Generative AI for Business Decision-Making

I am a graduate student at Johns Hopkins Carey Business School with a strong interest in AI, business analytics, and machine learning. My undergraduate background is mainly in business and mathematics, and although I only had basic experience with programming, I have recently developed a strong interest in generative AI. One area I care deeply about is business analytics and data-driven strategy. In many companies, large amounts of data are collected every day, but turning that data into clear and actionable insights is still a challenge. Bridging that gap—between raw data and confident decisions—is one of the defining challenges of modern business, and I believe AI has a central role to play in solving it.

**Three ways generative AI can help:**
- **Accelerating insight generation:** AI can summarize complex datasets, surface trends, and draft analytical narratives in a fraction of the time it would take a human analyst.
- **Enhancing decision support:** AI assistants can model scenarios, flag risks, and present trade-offs clearly, giving decision-makers better context without requiring deep technical expertise.
- **Automating routine reporting:** Repetitive reporting tasks—weekly dashboards, stakeholder updates, variance analyses—can be generated automatically, freeing analysts to focus on higher-value work.
